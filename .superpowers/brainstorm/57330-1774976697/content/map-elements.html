Which map elements matter most for the poster aesthetic?

Select all that feel essential for v1. These are the raw ingredients the app extracts from the map and lets users style.

1

Street Network

Roads, highways, alleys — the skeleton of a place. Different widths for major vs minor streets. This is what most map posters are built on.

2

Water Bodies

Rivers, lakes, coastlines, harbors. Filled shapes that create strong negative space and instantly make a map recognizable.

3

Parks & Green Space

Parks, gardens, forests. Adds organic shapes and a second layer of fill that breaks up the street grid.

4

Building Footprints

Individual building outlines. Adds incredible density and texture but can be visually noisy. Works best at neighborhood scale.

5

Terrain / Elevation

Hillshading, contour lines, elevation data. Adds topographic character but increases complexity significantly.

6

Place Labels / Street Names

Actual text from the map — neighborhood names, street labels. Can add authenticity but also visual clutter.

7

Rail & Transit Lines

Subway lines, train tracks, tram routes. Distinctive geometry that's iconic in cities like NYC, London, Tokyo.
